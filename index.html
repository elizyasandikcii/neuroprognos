What is Huntington's Disease?

Huntington's Disease (HD) is a progressive, inherited neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement, cognition, and psychiatric function. It's caused by a genetic mutation in the HTT gene, which leads to the production of an abnormal huntingtin protein that damages nerve cells in the brain.

Who Gets HD?

HD affects approximately 5-10 people per 100,000. It's an autosomal dominant disorder, meaning each child of an affected parent has a 50% chance of inheriting the gene.

Age of Onset

Symptoms typically appear between ages 30-50, though juvenile onset (before age 20) and late onset (after age 60) can occur. Earlier onset is associated with higher CAG repeat counts.

Disease Progression

HD progresses over 10-25 years, with symptoms gradually worsening over time. The disease has distinct stages: premanifest, early, moderate, and advanced.

Brain Changes

HD primarily affects the basal ganglia, particularly the caudate nucleus and putamen. These brain regions are involved in movement control, cognition, and emotion regulation.

How Do We Assess HD Risk?

Our assessment combines multiple validated approaches to provide accurate risk stratification:

Genetic Testing & CAG Analysis

The definitive method for HD diagnosis. CAG repeat length is the primary genetic determinant:

  • Normal: <36 CAG repeats
  • Reduced Penetrance: 36-39 repeats
  • Full Penetrance: â‰¥40 repeats

Higher CAG repeats correlate with earlier onset and more rapid progression.

Clinical Assessment Tools

Standardized scales used by neurologists:

  • UHDRS: Unified Huntington's Disease Rating Scale
  • TMS: Total Motor Score (0-124 scale)
  • TFC: Total Functional Capacity
  • SDMT: Symbol Digit Modalities Test (cognitive)

Predictive Models

Evidence-based algorithms that combine multiple factors:

  • Langbehn Formula: Age + CAG repeat prediction
  • CAP Score: CAG-Age Product for progression
  • Disease Burden Score: Age Ã— (CAG - 35.5)
  • Bayesian Risk Models: For unknown CAG status

Biomarkers & Imaging

Advanced assessment methods:

  • MRI Volumetry: Caudate nucleus volume loss
  • CSF Biomarkers: Neurofilament light chain (NfL)
  • PET Imaging: Metabolic changes in basal ganglia
  • Digital Biomarkers: Wearable sensor data

How Our Calculator Works

NeuroPrognos uses a sophisticated multi-factor approach:

Symptom Weighting

Different symptoms carry different weights based on HD specificity and impact on daily function. Motor symptoms are weighted most heavily.

Risk Stratification

We combine genetic data with symptom profiles to place individuals in appropriate risk categories: Low, Medium, or High risk.

Evidence-Based Recommendations

Treatment suggestions are based on clinical trial data and HDSA guidelines, with evidence levels (A, B, C) indicating scientific support.

Progression Prediction

Using TRACK-HD and PREDICT-HD data, we estimate likely symptom progression over 10 years based on current profile.

Symptom-Specific Models

Different symptom patterns are weighted based on HD-specific clinical studies

Motor Symptom Weights

Chorea, dystonia, and gait issues weighted based on UHDRS motor subscale studies.
